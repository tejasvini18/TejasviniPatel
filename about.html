My story

How I think about quality

Quality is not just “no bugs.” It’s about how a system behaves under pressure, how it fails, how it recovers, and how predictable it feels to the people who rely on it.

I design tests to reflect real users, real data, and real constraints—so stakeholders can make decisions with confidence, not guesswork.

How I work with teams

I value open communication, clear expectations, and shared ownership. I ask questions early, surface risks transparently, and keep the focus on outcomes, not blame.

My goal is to make developers feel supported, product owners feel informed, and users feel safe.

Principles I work by

These guide every test plan, every scenario, every release.

Exhaustive thinking

Edge cases, negative paths, and real‑world constraints are part of the plan, not an afterthought.

Calm under pressure

When timelines compress, I stay structured—prioritizing risk and impact, not noise.

Empathy for users

Every defect is a story about a user who could be blocked, confused, or exposed.

Continuous learning

New tools, new frameworks, new threats—I treat them as opportunities to sharpen my craft.
